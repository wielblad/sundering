# 1. VISION OF THE WORLD

## Tone and Atmosphere

The world of SUNDERING is a realm where **magic and steel** collide in an apocalyptic struggle for survival. This is not a world of black and white - both sides fight for what they believe to be righteous. The Mystic Forces protect primordial magic from annihilation, while the Human Alliance defends the right of mortals to self-determination.

### Key Narrative Elements:
- **Tragedy** - every hero carries the burden of their past
- **Honor** - even enemies respect worthy adversaries
- **Sacrifice** - victory demands offering
- **Mystery** - ancient secrets await discovery
- **Hope** - even in the darkest hour, a spark still burns

### Linguistic Style:
- Poetic yet accessible
- Archaisms used sparingly
- Metaphors tied to elements, nature, warfare
- Dialogue sharp yet meaningful

# 2. ANCIENT HISTORY

## The Era of First Dawn (10,000 years before the conflict)

When the world was young, the **Ancients** - beings of pure magical essence - shaped the lands from chaos. They forged mountains from frozen tears, oceans from spilled moonlight, and forests from the breath of wind. Magic was not a tool - it was the very fabric of reality.

The Ancients also created the **Nexus** - the heart of the world, a point where all lines of power converged. The Nexus was not a structure - it was a living gateway between worlds, the wellspring of all magic.

## The Era of Awakening (8,000 years before the conflict)

From the dust of the earth arose the **first humans**. Initially they lived in harmony with magic, and the Ancients observed them with fascination. Humans were... different. Short-lived yet full of fire. Weak yet unyielding. Devoid of magic yet capable of... something the Ancients could not comprehend.

**Creation.**

Humans built. Invented. Transcended the boundaries of what was possible. Where the Ancients meditated upon perfection for millennia, humans raised cities in mere decades.

## The Era of First Schism (5,000 years before the conflict)

The first conflict erupted when humans discovered how to **extract** magic from the earth. The first essence mines were established, and with them - the first wounds in the fabric of the world.

The Mystic Council warned. Humans did not listen.

Thus was born the **First Pact** - an agreement between the Mystic Council and the Kingdoms of Men:
- Humans may use magic but not extract it from the earth
- The Ancients shall not interfere in mortal affairs
- The Nexus remains neutral territory

The Pact endured for thousands of years. But all pacts are eventually broken.

## The Era of the Fall (1,000 years before the conflict)

Human civilization reached its zenith of power. The **Solar Empire** stretched from the frozen wastes of the north to the scorched deserts of the south. But human ambition knew no bounds.

The Architects of the Empire discovered the **Ancient Truth**: the Nexus was not merely a source of magic. It was the key to immortality.

Emperor Valdris III issued his command: seize the Nexus.

The **Battle for the Heart of the World** raged for seven days and seven nights. The armies of the Empire, armed with anti-magic weaponry, pierced the defenses of the Ancients for the first time. But the price was... unimaginable.

When the imperial general drove his blade into the core of the Nexus, the world **groaned**. Half the continent collapsed into the abyss. Millions perished. The Empire fell in a single day.

And the Nexus... the Nexus survived. But it was wounded. And within that wound... something awakened.

## The Era of Silence (1,000 years of stillness)

A thousand years passed in shadow. Humans rebuilt civilization from ashes, trying to forget their hubris. The Ancients withdrew to the depths of the world, tending their wounds.

But in the darkness, within the Fracture of the Nexus, the **Shadow Plague** grew - a corruption of magic itself. Slowly, imperceptibly, it poisoned everything it touched.

## The Era of Awakening (Present Day)

Three hundred years ago, the first signs of the Plague reached the surface. Animals mutated. Forests died. Magic began to... go mad.

The **Mystic Council** made a desperate decision: awaken the slumbering Ancients to cleanse the Nexus from within. But this required energy. Immense energy. Energy that could only be harvested from living beings.

Humans call it the **Reaping**. Mystical entities summoned by the Council who take souls to feed the ritual of purification.

The **Human Alliance** sees it differently: this is an invasion. Annihilation. And they resolved to defend themselves at any cost - even if it means destroying magic itself.

Thus began the **Eternal Conflict**.

# 3. FACTIONS AND THEIR PHILOSOPHIES

## MYSTIC FORCES

### Philosophy: "Magic Is Life"
For the Mystics, magic is not a tool - it is the very essence of existence. Without magic, the world will become an empty shell, a place where nothing blooms, nothing dreams, nothing truly lives.

### Structure:
- **The Mystic Council** - five Ancients who survived the Fall
- **Guardians of the Elements** - elementals of fire, water, earth, air
- **Spirits of Nature** - beings bound to forests, rivers, mountains
- **The Exiled** - those who turned against their own kind (like Scourge)

### War Objective:
Cleanse the Nexus, even if it requires the sacrifice of human civilization. In their eyes, this is not evil - it is surgical amputation to save the body.

### Tragedy:
Many among the Mystics remember the times when humans and magic lived in harmony. Some (like Sylara or Zephyr) believe this harmony can be restored. But they are in the minority.

---

## HUMAN ALLIANCE

### Philosophy: "The Future Belongs to Us"
Humans did not choose to be mortals. Did not choose to be weaker than the Ancients. But they chose not to go gently into that good night. If magic means their annihilation - they will destroy magic.

### Structure:
- **The War Council** - generals of the seven Free Cities
- **The Order of Dawn** - knights who battle magic
- **The Guild of Artificers** - creators of anti-magic technology
- **The Free Hunters** - mercenaries who hunt mystical creatures

### War Objective:
Destroy or secure the Nexus from the Mystic Council. They believe a controlled Nexus can grant humanity immortality without annihilation.

### Tragedy:
Many humans hate magic because they lost loved ones to the Shadow Plague or the Reaping. But there are also those like Sylara (yes - she is a nature spirit healing for the alliance, though she serves as the human healer), who remember that not all mystical beings are enemies.

# 4. WORLD MAP

## The Battlefield - The Blood Fields of the Nexus

### Center: THE NEXUS
The pulsing heart of the world. A crystalline tower rising toward the heavens, entwined with veins of pure energy. At its base - the **Fracture** - the wound inflicted a thousand years ago, from which the Shadow Plague seeps.

### Three Paths (Lanes):

**UPPER PATH - The Iron Trail**
Ruins of the ancient Imperial forge. Here, anti-magic blades were wrought. The earth is dead, marked with metallic streaks. The ghosts of smiths still forge invisible blades.

**MIDDLE PATH - The Road of Bones**
The most direct route to the Nexus, but also the most perilous. Here lie the bones of millions who fell in the Battle for the Heart of the World. They say that at night, the bones whisper.

**LOWER PATH - The Drowning Mire**
Once forests, now submerged in toxic swamp. The Shadow Plague corrupted the waters here. Mushrooms grow here, glowing with pale light - the only source of orientation in the eternal fog.

### Jungle:

**NORTHERN JUNGLE - The Beast Graveyard**
Home to wolves, golems, and other creatures. Corrupted by the Plague, but still alive.

**SOUTHERN JUNGLE - The Cursed Thickets**
Here lurk harpies and centaurs. Trees grow inverted - roots reaching skyward.

### River:

**THE RIVER OF SOULS**
Flows through the center of the battlefield. Its waters glow with a pale, bluish light - the souls of the fallen who cannot find peace. Drinking from it grants visions of past and future... and madness.

### Special Locations:

**THE DRAGON'S LAIR**
Deep within the southern jungle. Home of the Elder Dragon - the only being who remembers the times before the Ancients. Whoever defeats it gains its blessing.

**THE ANCIENT ALTAR**
In the center of the river. Whoever makes an offering here may summon the spirits of ancestors for a brief moment.

# 5. HEROES - DETAILED PROFILES

## HUMAN FACTION

### IRONCLAD - The Unyielding Shield

**True Name:** Torvin Ironhorn

**Age:** 47 years (appears 35 - a side effect of the armor he wears)

**Origin:** The Northern Citadel - a fortress forged into an icy peak

**History:**
Torvin was born the son of a blacksmith in the Northern Citadel. From childhood he was larger and stronger than his peers, but also more... deliberate. Not stupid - he simply thought before he acted. In a world where everyone rushed, Torvin was a rock.

When he was twenty years old, the Citadel was attacked by the **Shadow Horde** - an army of demons pouring forth from the Fracture of the Nexus. Torvin stood at the Northern Gate for three days and three nights. Not because he was a hero. Because there was nowhere to retreat.

His armor - forged by his father - absorbed so much demonic energy during the battle that it fused with his body. Now Torvin cannot remove it. Nor can he die of old age. He is a prisoner of his own defense.

**Character:**
- Silent, but when he speaks - people listen
- Chronic pessimist ("We will all die. But not today.")
- Secretly writes poetry (poorly)
- Hates magic but respects magical warriors

**Quote:**
*"A shield does not win battles. A shield buys time. And sometimes time is all those behind you need."*

**Relationships:**
- **Bladewarden:** Irritating chatterbox. But in battle, there is none better.
- **Valeria:** Too much faith, not enough common sense.
- **Pyralis:** Respects him. The fire in him and in Torvin is similar - both burn so that others need not.

---

### BLADEWARDEN - The Storm of Steel

**True Name:** Kael Two-Blades

**Age:** 34 years

**Origin:** The Free City of Argentum - capital of gladiators

**History:**
Kael was a child of the streets. He knew no parents - they were likely slaves who perished in the arena. He raised himself stealing bread and learning to fight with other homeless children.

At the age of fourteen, he was caught and sold to the arena. He was meant to be fodder - a boy for a gladiator to kill as a warm-up. But Kael had other plans.

Over the next ten years, he won **two hundred and forty-two duels**. He earned the title of Blade Master, his freedom, and two swords forged from a meteorite - **Grace** and **Vengeance**.

And then he discovered that the owner of the arena - the man who had bought him as a child - was his father.

Kael left Argentum that same night. Now he fights in the Eternal Conflict, seeking redemption for all those he killed for the entertainment of the crowd.

**Character:**
- Seemingly carefree and talkative
- Beneath the surface: immense guilt
- Never strikes first - waits for the opponent to begin
- Has a nightmare in which the faces of defeated gladiators ask "why?"

**Quote:**
*"Two blades. One for grace, one for vengeance. I always offer a choice. They always choose vengeance."*

**Relationships:**
- **Ironclad:** The only one beside whom he can be silent without guilt.
- **Vex:** Recognizes the street rat in her. Tries to protect her from her own thirst for revenge.
- **Nightshade:** Former rival from the arena. She went to the shadows - he went to the light.

---

### SYLARA - The Giver of Life

**True Name:** Sylara of the Eternal Grove

**Age:** Unknown (appears 25, but remembers the First Pact)

**Origin:** The Eternal Grove - a forest that exists in no particular place

**History:**
Sylara is a **paradox**. A spirit of nature who fights alongside humans against her own kin.

She was born when the first ray of sunlight touched the first leaf of the first tree. She is older than most Ancients. She remembers the times when humans and magic lived in symbiosis.

When the Mystic Council declared the Reaping, Sylara objected. She argued that humans are not a disease - they are part of the world as real as magic. For her defiance, she was exiled.

Now she heals human warriors, believing her mercy can show both sides that coexistence is possible.

**Character:**
- Patient as a forest (literally)
- Deeply sad, but does not show it
- Believes in the goodness of every being
- Has a tendency to speak in riddles

**Quote:**
*"Healing is not giving life. It is reminding the body that it wishes to live."*

**Relationships:**
- **Thornweaver:** Former friend from the Grove. Now an enemy. This hurts most of all.
- **Zephyr:** The only Mystic who understands her. They meet in secret.
- **Grimjaw:** His simplicity fascinates her - he does not seek meaning, he simply is.

---

### VEX - The Venomous Huntress

**True Name:** Unknown (she abandoned it long ago)

**Age:** 23 years

**Origin:** The Slums of the Forsaken - the district beneath the Free City of Argentum

**History:**
Vex was born in a place where sunlight does not reach. Underground tunnels beneath the arena, where those nobody wants live - the crippled, widows of gladiators, children of slaves.

Her mother was a healer in the slums - the only person who truly tried to help. When the Shadow Plague reached the tunnels, it killed hundreds. Vex's mother worked day and night until she herself fell victim.

Vex was twelve years old. She decided that the world that allowed her mother to die would pay.

Over the following years, she became one of the most effective assassins in the Slums. Her specialty: poisons extracted from mushrooms growing on the remains of Plague victims. The irony of using the disease that killed her mother as a weapon is not lost on her.

**Character:**
- Cold and calculating on the outside
- Inside: immense rage and sorrow
- Kills quickly and cleanly - dislikes suffering (she has seen too much herself)
- Has a weakness for street children - anonymously leaves them gold

**Quote:**
*"Venom is an honest death. Swift. Silent. More than most victims ever received from life."*

**Relationships:**
- **Bladewarden:** Tries to convert her. Irritating. But... kind?
- **Nightshade:** Rivalry. Both are killers, but from different worlds.
- **Hex:** Similar traumas, different responses. Vex kills the body, Hex - the soul.

### MAGNUS - The Master of Gravity

**True Name:** Magister Aurelius Graviton

**Age:** 67 years (appears 40 - gravity slows aging)

**Origin:** The Academy of Seven Towers - humanity's highest institution of magic

**History:**
Aurelius was a genius. Literally. At the age of six, he solved the Paradox of the Third Moon. At twelve, he discovered that gravity is not a force - it is a curvature of magic.

At twenty years old, he accidentally created a **micro-black hole** that consumed an entire wing of the Academy. Forty magisters perished. Including his mentor and only friend.

Aurelius spent the next forty years trying to reverse his mistake. He failed. But in the process, he became the most powerful gravity mage in history.

He fights in the Eternal Conflict not out of hatred for magic - but because he believes a controlled Nexus will finally allow him to turn back time and undo his error.

**Character:**
- Painfully eccentric
- Talks to himself (and to gravity - claims it responds)
- Brilliant but socially awkward
- Haunted by visions of those he killed

**Quote:**
*"Gravity does not attract. Space bends. Everything merely falls into the pit it created for itself."*

**Relationships:**
- **Pyralis:** Fascinating thermodynamic equations. Pyralis hates him.
- **Frostborne:** Theoretical discussions about the physics of magic. The only one who understands him.
- **Hex:** Avoids her. Curses disrupt equations.

### GRIMJAW - The Berserker

**True Name:** Grok (no surname - barbarians do not use them)

**Age:** 35 years (or 40, he does not remember)

**Origin:** The Tribe of Blood Snow - nomads from the Frozen Wastes

**History:**
Grok was born during a storm that killed half the tribe. The shaman deemed it an omen - the child would be either a destroyer or a savior. Nothing in between.

Grok chose the first option.

At the age of sixteen, he killed his first demon with his bare hands. He literally tore out its jaw and wears it to this day as a trophy (hence the name "Grimjaw").

His secret: the more he bleeds, the stronger he becomes. This is not magic - it is a mutation caused by the Shadow Plague. Grok's blood is poisonous to himself, but in battle it releases hormones that make him nearly indestructible.

**Character:**
- Simple as an axe
- Does not understand subtlety (literally - not arrogance, it simply escapes him)
- Surprisingly loyal to companions
- Deep down, fears dying alone, far from the snows

**Quote:**
*"Talking too long. Fighting quick. Grok prefers fight."*

**Relationships:**
- **Ironclad:** The only one who matches him in close combat. Warrior's respect.
- **Sylara:** She healed him once. Grok does not understand why a forest spirit helps humans. But he is grateful.
- **Scourge:** Hatred. Scourge is a demon. Grok kills demons.

---

### VALERIA - The Radiant Shield

**True Name:** Dame Valeria of the Order of Dawn

**Age:** 29 years

**Origin:** The Monastery of Radiant Dawn - a fortress-temple upon the Hill of First Morning

**History:**
Valeria was an orphan abandoned at the gates of the monastery. The monks raised her as a warrior-priestess, teaching that light is the only true weapon against darkness.

At the age of twenty, during the Rite of Purification, Valeria experienced... something. A vision. A future in which the Shadow Plague consumes everything. And a voice that said: "Only a pure heart can close the Fracture."

Valeria believes she is chosen. Some call it arrogance. She calls it a burden.

Her greatest secret: during the vision, she also felt something else. Warmth. Familiar. And she heard a second word - "daughter." Her mother lives. And she is on the side of the Mystics.

**Character:**
- Unwavering faith (sometimes blind)
- Strict but just
- Does not kill if she need not
- Battles doubts every night

**Quote:**
*"Faith is not certainty. Faith is fighting despite the doubts."*

**Relationships:**
- **Ironclad:** Considers him too cynical. But respects his sacrifice.
- **Pyralis:** An enemy, but... an honest one. She would rather convert him than kill him.
- **Zephyr:** A mysterious feeling that the wind spirit knows something about her past.

---

### HEX - The Weaver of Curses

**True Name:** Elara Thrice-Burned

**Age:** Unknown (appears anywhere from 30-50 depending on her mood)

**Origin:** The Village of Frost's Edge - burned by the Order of Dawn for "heresy"

**History:**
Elara was an ordinary herbalist. She healed the sick, assisted at births, conversed with cats (they answered - that is normal for herbalists).

And then the Order of Dawn came.

They accused her of consorting with darkness. They burned her home. They burned her cats. They burned her.

But Elara did not die. She rose from the ashes. And they burned her again.

She rose a second time.

The third time, they used holy fire - fire meant to purify the soul. And... it worked. Some part of Elara truly died that day.

What rose from the ashes the third time was no longer Elara. It was **Hex** - a being of hatred and bitterness who learned to curse so effectively that the Order itself fears her.

She fights on the side of humans only because the Mystic Forces refused her vengeance against the Order. The enemy of my enemy...

**Character:**
- Sarcastic to a painful degree
- Hates everyone equally (almost)
- Has moments of strange tenderness - rescues animals
- Probably mad (but can one blame her?)

**Quote:**
*"Three times you burned me. Now I shall burn your world. Slowly. Curse by curse."*

**Relationships:**
- **Valeria:** Hatred so pure it is almost beautiful. Valeria represents everything Hex lost.
- **Sylara:** The only one who tries to speak with her. Hex hates her for it. And loves her. It is complicated.
- **Vex:** Kindred souls. Different poisons.

## MYSTIC FACTION

### PYRALIS - The Flame Incarnate

**True Name:** None. Fire needs no name.

**Age:** Has existed since the first flame burst from the heart of the world.

**Origin:** The Heart of the Volcano of First Fire

**History:**
Pyralis is an **elemental** - an essence of fire that gained consciousness. For thousands of years he slumbered in the heart of the volcano, dreaming dreams of smoke and ash.

The Mystic Council awakened him when the Shadow Plague began extinguishing the fires of the world. Pyralis watched as flames died one by one - hearths went cold without reason, forges grew still, the sun grew pale.

For a being of fire, this was the apocalypse. And Pyralis resolved to burn everything necessary to save flame itself.

But fire is... unpredictable. Sometimes Pyralis feels something he does not understand - moments when he does not wish to destroy. Moments when warmth is more pleasant than scorching heat. Humans are a strange magic.

**Character:**
- Impulsive (literally - he is a flame)
- Fascinated by humans - so fragile, yet so hot within
- Incapable of lying - fire does not lie
- Sometimes... lonely. Fire needs fuel, and eventually consumes everything around it.

**Quote:**
*"Fire does not hate the wood it burns. Such is its nature. Such is mine."*

**Relationships:**
- **Frostborne:** Opposite. Hatred? No. Fascination. How can fire hate ice when without it, it would never know what warmth is?
- **Nightshade:** Shadow and fire. She extinguishes him, he illuminates her. A dance.
- **Valeria:** Her "holy fire" offends him. That is not fire - it is a lie.

---

### NIGHTSHADE - The Reaper of Shadows

**True Name:** Unknown (they say speaking it aloud summons death)

**Age:** Unknown (shadows do not measure time)

**Origin:** The Realm of Shadows - a dimension between worlds

**History:**
Nightshade was not born. She was **created** - forged from shadow itself by the Lords of Darkness, ancient beings older even than the Ancients.

From her first moment of awareness, she was trained as the perfect killer. Not a murderer - murderers have emotions. Nightshade was... a tool. A blade. A shadow that takes life and returns to the dark.

But during one mission... she saw something she should not have. A child - a human child - crying over the body of its mother. Nightshade's target had just... ceased to be a target.

She hesitated.

The Lords of Darkness do not tolerate hesitation. They exiled her, condemning her to existence between worlds - too real for the Realm of Shadows, too dark for the world of the living.

Now she serves the Mystic Council because it is the only place where she can exist. But sometimes, in solitude, she wonders what would have happened if that day... she had not hesitated.

**Character:**
- Cold and impersonal on the outside
- Inside: chaos of questions without answers
- Kills quickly and cleanly - considers prolonging death to be cruelty
- Has a weakness for children (never kills them)

**Quote:**
*"Shadow is not the absence of light. Shadow is what light leaves behind."*

**Relationships:**
- **Bladewarden:** They remember each other from the arena. She went to the shadows, he went to the light. Both wonder if they chose rightly.
- **Pyralis:** Avoids him. Fire hurts.
- **Vex:** Respect between killers. Different methods, similar souls.

### THORNWEAVER - The Living Fortress

**True Name:** Rowan of a Thousand Roots

**Age:** 847 years

**Origin:** The Primordial Grove - a forest older than human memory

**History:**
Rowan was once a druid. A human druid. He worshipped nature, spoke with trees, protected the forest from all that threatened it.

When the Solar Empire marched upon the Nexus, their armies passed through the Primordial Grove. The druids tried to stop them - and were slaughtered. All of them. Except for Rowan.

Dying, Rowan embraced the oldest tree in the grove - the Great Oak - and begged for strength to avenge himself upon the human invaders.

The Oak answered.

Over the following eight centuries, Rowan and the Oak became one. His body is intertwined roots and branches. His blood is sap. His heart is the core of a tree that remembers everything.

He hates humans. Every one. Without exception. Even those who serve nature. Because he was one of them. And he knows how easily they betray.

**Character:**
- Stern and merciless
- Speaks slowly, as if every word must pass through ages
- Despises human "brevity of life"
- Deep within - infinitely weary, but too stubborn to cease

**Quote:**
*"Eight centuries. That was enough for the forest to forget human treachery. But I remember. And I will never forget."*

**Relationships:**
- **Sylara:** Former friend from the Grove. Now a traitor. Her choice pains him more than he will admit.
- **Grimjaw:** A barbarian from the north. A savage. But... honest. Thornweaver hates that he respects him.
- **Ironclad:** Sees in him a reflection of himself - a warrior who became a prisoner of his own defense.

### FROSTBORNE - The Frozen Heart

**True Name:** Isira of the Eternal Glaciers

**Age:** Unknown (ice does not measure time)

**Origin:** The Glacier of First Frost

**History:**
Isira was once human. A girl from a northern village who fell in love with the chieftain's son.

When the Great Winter came - a winter that lasted seven years - the village began dying of hunger. The elders made a decision: offer one soul to the Spirit of Winter in exchange for salvation.

The lot fell upon Isira.

They bound her to the glacier and left her. But Isira did not die. The Spirit of Winter - an ancient being of cold - saw something in her. Strength. Determination. And offered her a choice: death by warmth or life in cold.

Isira chose the cold.

Now she is an elemental of ice. She remembers warmth - theoretically. But she cannot feel it. Every touch burns her. Every closeness is... impossible.

She fights in the Eternal Conflict because it is all that remains to her. And because somewhere, in a heart frozen for ages, she still waits for something that will warm her without burning.

**Character:**
- Calm as the surface of a frozen lake
- Speaks quietly, but every word carries weight
- Fascinated by emotions - she herself cannot fully feel them
- A mysterious longing for something she does not remember

**Quote:**
*"Ice is not the absence of warmth. Ice is patience frozen in waiting."*

**Relationships:**
- **Pyralis:** Opposite. Attraction. Repulsion. A dance that has lasted for ages.
- **Magnus:** The only one who treats her as an equal - intellectually, not emotionally.
- **Valeria:** Intriguing. Her faith is... warm. Isira wishes to understand.

### ZEPHYR - The Spirit of Wind

**True Name:** Zephyr (the wind does not lie about its name)

**Age:** As old as the first breath of the world, as young as the last breeze

**Origin:** Nowhere and Everywhere - the wind has no home

**History:**
Zephyr is **wind**. Not a spirit of wind - the wind itself, which gained consciousness when the first mortal whispered a wish into a storm.

For millennia he observed the world from on high. He watched empires rise and fall. He witnessed every battle, every love, every loss. Wind is everywhere, and Zephyr... Zephyr listens.

Unlike other elementals, Zephyr does not wish to destroy. He prefers to whisper rather than scream. To lift rather than cast down. To... help.

He joined the Eternal Conflict not out of loyalty to the Council, but out of curiosity. And because... he likes humans. They are so brief and intense. Like a summer storm.

**Character:**
- Playful and light (literally)
- Incapable of staying in one place
- Collects stories - asks everyone he meets for a tale
- A secret romantic - believes in love more than in war

**Quote:**
*"The wind does not remember where it blows from. It remembers only where it carries."*

**Relationships:**
- **Sylara:** Close friends. They meet in secret to dream of a world without war.
- **Valeria:** Knows something about her past. Does not speak. Yet.
- **Grimjaw:** Enjoys annoying him by snatching his things. Grok hates the wind.

### SCOURGE - The Demon Prince

**True Name:** Malachar, the Third Evil

**Age:** Older than sin, younger than redemption

**Origin:** The Infernal Abyss - a dimension of destruction

**History:**
Malachar was a **prince** of the Abyss. Third of seven sons of the Lord of Demons. He had everything - power, armies, an eternity of suffering to inflict.

But Malachar wanted something more. He wanted... to feel.

Demons do not feel. Hatred is not an emotion - it is a state of being. Malachar observed mortals and saw something he did not understand. Joy. Sorrow. Love.

He began to experiment. He released victims. Healed wounds he had inflicted. Tried to... be good.

His father does not tolerate weakness. Malachar was exiled, stripped of most of his power, condemned to existence between worlds.

He joined the Eternal Conflict because... battle is all he knows. And because perhaps, just perhaps, in the chaos of war he will find the answer to the question that haunts him: can a demon cease to be a demon?

**Character:**
- Brutal yet strangely honorable
- Never lies (demons cannot)
- Fascinated by human emotions
- Deep within: desperate hope for redemption

**Quote:**
*"I betrayed the Abyss for this world. I will betray this world for answers. I will betray everything to feel something real."*

**Relationships:**
- **Grimjaw:** Mutual hatred. Grok kills demons. Scourge is a demon. Simple.
- **Hex:** Recognizes hell in her. Human hell. Fascinating.
- **Valeria:** Wants her to kill him with "holy fire." Perhaps it is the only way to find a true end?

## JUNGLE MONSTERS

### Wolves - The Hungering Pack
**Legend:** They were once ordinary wolves. The Shadow Plague transformed them into eternal hunters - never sated, always hungry. The Alpha Pack remembers the times before the Plague and howls for the peace it has lost.

### Golems - The Core Guardians
**Legend:** The Ancients created them as laborers - beings of stone that built the world. Now they defend what remains. The Elder Golem remembers every stone it laid. Each one aches.

**Legend:** They were angels. Truly. They served beings higher even than the Ancients. When their masters departed, the harpies went mad with grief. Now they scream songs that no one understands.

### Centaurs - The Exiles of the Steppes
**Legend:** The last tribe of free riders. When the world changed, the centaurs refused to choose a side. As punishment, they were imprisoned in the jungle - a place without open space.

### The Elder Dragon (Elder Dragon)
**Legend:** The only being that remembers the times before the Ancients. They say it was he who first breathed fire into the heart of the world. Now he sleeps, awaiting the end. Whoever awakens him gains a fragment of his wisdom... or his madness.
